About Capital Language Schools

Capital Language Schools (CLS) is intentionally small. We are a remote‑first language school built for busy people who still want deep, human learning—not generic apps or overcrowded classrooms. Lessons take place online in live sessions, so you can join from wherever you are without needing a physical campus.

Instead of trying to cover every language, we focus on a handful we know we can teach exceptionally well. At the moment, that means English, Spanish, and French, with occasional seasonal workshops in other languages. Fewer options means more attention to materials, teaching style, and the real goals our learners bring with them.

Classes are deliberately small, so your microphone is never just “on mute.” You spend most of the lesson speaking, listening, and getting gentle, practical feedback. Homework is designed to fit into real routines—voice notes, short reading, and quick practice tasks you can do between calls rather than hours of worksheets.

Curriculums Designed Around Real Schedules

Our programs are simple on purpose. Instead of dozens of overlapping levels, you choose a track that fits how you live: steady weekly practice, short sprints for an upcoming move or exam, or one‑to‑one coaching when you need something very specific. Our classes are online, so your “classroom” can be your desk, kitchen table, or favorite café.

Each curriculum blends live conversation, guided listening, and a small amount of reading and writing. We use authentic materials—short clips, real messages, and simple articles—so what you learn in class actually sounds like the language people use outside of it.

Starter Sessions

Ideal for brand‑new learners or people coming back after a long break. Starter Sessions help you build a gentle base in sounds, basic phrases, and real situations: introducing yourself, talking about your day, or asking for what you need.

  • Beginner and false‑beginner levels
  • Short, focused classes that reduce overwhelm
  • Friendly correction and simple take‑home practice

Conversation Circles

Small‑group conversation designed to get you talking more naturally. Each session centers on one clear theme—like travel, work calls, or daily routines—so you can relax into speaking while still learning new words and patterns.

  • Groups of 6–8 learners maximum
  • Rotating topics with repeatable language
  • Teacher‑guided, but learner‑driven conversations

Focused Coaching

One‑to‑one sessions for specific goals: an upcoming interview, a move abroad, a conference talk, or simply a part of the language you keep getting stuck on. Your coach builds a short, targeted plan around exactly what you need.

  • Flexible scheduling for busy weeks
  • Personalized materials and practice
  • Clear, trackable mini‑goals each week

Education at Capital Language Schools

Our education system follows a clear path: KG & Primary, Preparatory & Secondary, and a flexible Online System that supports learners and families at every stage. Explore each division below to see how we shape language learning for different ages and needs.

KG & Primary

In KG and Primary, the goal is to make language feel playful and natural. Learners sing, act out stories, and move around while they speak. Sessions are short, visual, and full of repetition so children can build confidence without pressure.

Parents receive simple suggestions for at‑home practice—like short games, key phrases, and little rituals they can repeat each week. The focus is on curiosity, sounds, and basic communication, not tests.

Preparatory & Secondary

Preparatory and Secondary learners start using language for real tasks: school projects, exams, and everyday communication with friends or teachers. Lessons mix speaking and writing, with small projects that feel real—presentations, debates, and simple research tasks.

Teachers help students prepare for school assessments without turning every class into exam practice. Grammar and vocabulary are always tied to what they actually need to say and write in their lives right now.

Online System

Our online system ties everything together. Learners join live sessions in small groups of 20–25, with clear routines so they know what to expect each time. Materials and homework are shared through simple online tools that work on phones, tablets, and laptops.

Families and adult learners can track progress through short written updates and occasional feedback calls. Because the school is fully online, it is easier to adjust schedules, move between groups, and keep learning steady even when life is busy or you need to travel.
